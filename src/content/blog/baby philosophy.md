# baby philosophy
Sometimes life feels crammed with trivialities, and many times I find myself tripping over simple questions: What is life? What is love? 

Like what a modern human would do in the post-AI era, I go to the chatbot for quick answers, and asked them to answer from a baby's perspective. Answers I got are pretty satisfactory... at least to me. 

Date : June 22, 2025

If I were a newborn baby (with somehow the ability to speak or comprehend philosophy!), my response to *"What is love?"* might be something like this:
*"Waaah! Warm arms. Milk. Soft sounds. Bright eyes looking at me. No hunger. No cold. No fear. Smell of someone who stays. Heartbeat near mine. That?"*
(Translation: Love is safety, presence, and the primal bond that meets needs and whispers, *"You’re not alone."*)
Of course, a real newborn would just stare, cry, or yawn—but it’s fun to imagine. 😄


If I were a newborn baby (with magical philosophical clarity!), my answer to *“What is life?”* might sound like this:
**"Bright! Loud! Cold air—then warm. Faces blur. Hunger… then full. Tired… then sleep. Hands hold me. Voices hum. Something in me *aches* to grow. What is *not* life?"**
(Translation: Life is sensation, need, cycles of comfort/discomfort, and the raw pulse of becoming. To a baby, it’s the first breath, the first bond, and the instinct to reach toward light.)
A real infant, of course, would respond with a hiccup or a fist waved at the void. But metaphorically? Babies *are* life—pure, unfiltered, and hungry for it.

Later I came across the word biophilia, which I think abstracts the 'baby philosophy' well. In this AI era, as a human being, I’m always confused what it means to be human? And sometimes a solid answer like this makes me feel at least a bit more anchored in the storm.
